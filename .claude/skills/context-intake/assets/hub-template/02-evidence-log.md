# Evidence Log

Every meaningful claim anywhere in this hub should trace back to a row here with its
provenance, or be a labeled, traceable `Assumption`. Don't over-polish source material —
capture it raw.

## Sources

| Source | Provenance | Date | Pointer | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| | | | | |

A source's provenance is one of three (assumptions are claims, not sources — see below):
- `internal` — vision doc, PRD, meeting transcript, deck, Slack message/thread, email, internal decision
- `external` — competitor research (page, pricing, journey) or customer/market research (interview, review, win-loss note, support-ticket theme, product analytics, SEO/market signal)
- `product-observation` — hands-on use of our own product

## Extracted Claims

Format: `Claim text. [Confidence | prov: <type>: pointer]`

A claim's provenance is one of the three source types above, or
`assumption -> <internal|external pointer>` — a deliberate bet traced to the source that
motivated it. An assumption with no pointer is the one forbidden case: untraceable.

Confidence values: `Confirmed | Validated | Inferred | Assumption | Hypothesis | Contradicted | Needs-validation`
